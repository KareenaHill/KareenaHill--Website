Despite my best efforts to conserve money, my earnings as a young college student are continually declining. One of the many life lessons I've learned while attending college and as a young adult is how challenging it can be to integrate the demands of my financial obligations with those of my personal and academic obligations. I never seem to have a reliable income. Many students now find it very challenging to manage their finances, which has caused reductions in their academic performance, issues with their mental health, and unexpected compromises that shouldn't be forced onto them. In response to this, institutions do need to devise a solution that can teach students how to effectively manage their money while also providing options that are universally affordable and fall within the budget of college students, whether that be the cost of tuition, supplies, or even living to address this systemic issue. I am not the only student who struggles to meet basic needs; according to a recent study, at least 40% of college students struggle to pay for tuition and other expenses. 60% of college students, according to the same study, claimed they wouldn't even be able to cover unforeseen costs like car maintenance and medical bills (Leonhardt, M. 2020). To further clarify, the impact of such demanding charges causes students to struggle to maintain both their academic responsibilities and mental health.
